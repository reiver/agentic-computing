Agentic Computing

agency (noun): the ability to act independently, make decisions, and act toward a goal

by Charles Iliya Krempeaux (reiver)

The word “agentic” comes from the word “agency” — the ability to act independently, make decisions, and act toward a goal

A lot of traditional software waits some type of instruction (from a human) or some type of input before acting. For example:

  • When a person clicks a button → the software persons an action.
  • When a person asks a question → the software provides an answer.
  • When a person makes a request → the software provides a response.
  • Etc.

This is your e-mail application, your banking app, and your messaging software, etc.

With agentic computing (also sometimes called agentic AI or AI agents) software can:

  • Understand a goal.
  • Plan a sequence of actions
  • Use tools or resources.
  • Monitor progress.
  • Adjust their approach when conditions change.

Example

For example, a human might ask an agent:

Find me a flight to Isfahan next month, compare prices, and draft an itinerary.

And really, one agent could even ask another agent!

Capabilities

Here are the capabilities that agentic computing has:

Capability Meaning Example
Goal-Oriented Planning Dividing a larger goal into a sequence of sub-goals. I.e., the agent identifies what needs to be done and figures out steps/sub-goals to archive the desired outcome. The steps will often have a ordr or priority to them.
Tool Use Knowing how and when to use external software, APIs, databases, and other tools.
Memory Retaining short-term context of the current task and long-term user preferences.
Reflection & Correction The agent learns from its mistakes. It inspects its own output or the results, realizes when it makes a mistake, and changes its approach. This could perhaps be called: contiguous learning.
